About Me

I started coding in 2013 as I joined the University of Waterloo to study computer science. Luckly, I handled the dynamic days of programming pretty well that I had many opportunities to collaborate with great researchers and engineers all over the world. In 2018, I shifted my focus to deep learning and started publishing papers under the guidance of Jimmy Lin. Natural language processing and speech recognition were the main interests but I also explored generative adversarial networks and clustering. Currently, I am in the domain of computer vision building object detection models for robotic systems.
